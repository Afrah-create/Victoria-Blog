About me

Layered surfaces, social narratives, and emotional truth.

“Through this work, I aim to evoke empathy, awareness, and discomfort, the responses I think we need before anything can really change.”

I'm a fine art student working in the Exhibition course unit. My practice is built around painting as a way to reflect lived experiences, cultural narratives, and the contemporary realities I care about.

I use paint, texture, colour, and layered surfaces to carry emotion, ideas, and stories that link what I've lived with the wider communities I'm part of. Acrylics, oils, mixed media, and whatever tools fit the piece are how I shape that meaning.

When I apply, layer, and rework paint, I'm building visual narratives, traces of movement, memory, and identity that I hope others can feel as well as see.

Journal

Notes from the Studio

March 18, 2026

When Silence Becomes a Subject

While developing “Bound by Silence,” I returned to memories of fear and the quiet emotional weight many children carry.

What began as sketches in low light became larger passages of shadow. I kept asking how little detail could still read as panic. The belt and chains were never decorative. They had to feel unavoidable, the way silence often does for a child.

If the image unsettles you, it is doing part of its work. I paint so we can name what is often left unnamed.

February 9, 2026

Layering as Narrative

I have been testing acrylic and oil combinations to create surfaces that feel lived in.

Each layer records a decision, a pause, or a shift in how a story is told. I scrape back, glaze, and sometimes let a layer stay partially visible so earlier decisions haunt the surface. That mirrors how memory works in the studio. Nothing fully disappears.

The finished canvas should feel like it was lived through, not only planned.

January 22, 2026

Exhibition Unit Reflections

Working in the Exhibition course has sharpened my focus on communication.

I am thinking about how experiences rooted in community can be held with care inside a painting space. I am learning to write labels and wall text that welcome people in without flattening complex stories.

The unit pushes me to consider who walks through the door and what they are invited to carry away. Painting, for me, is one thread in that larger weave of showing and telling.
